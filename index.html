Abstract

Variational Quantum Algorithms (VQAs) are among the most promising approaches for near-term quantum advantage, yet their scalability is fundamentally limited by barren plateausâ€”exponentially vanishing gradients with increasing system size. This work introduces Hierarchical Contextual Global Optimization (HC-GO), a novel framework that simultaneously preserves true global cost functions, provably mitigates barren plateaus, and enables efficient measurement via classical shadow tomography.

HC-GO introduces a Quantum Context Register (QCR) that carries compressed global correlations through sequential isometric cells, allowing representation of long-range entanglement while confining optimization to polynomial-sized local subspaces. Theoretical guarantees are established via a PUSH-through operator demonstrating exact equivalence between global and compressed observables.
